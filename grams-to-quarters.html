About Weight Units

The gram (g) is a metric unit of weight defined as one thousandth of a kilogram. It's widely used in scientific contexts and in most countries worldwide.

The quarter (qtr) is an imperial unit of weight equal to 28 pounds or one-quarter of a hundredweight (112 pounds). It's equivalent to approximately 12.7 kilograms and was historically used for trade and commerce in the UK.
