About Weight Units

The gram (g) is a metric unit of weight defined as one thousandth of a kilogram. It's widely used in scientific contexts and in most countries worldwide.

The short ton is a unit of weight equal to 2,000 pounds or approximately 907.18 kilograms. It's commonly used in the United States for measuring large weights such as vehicles, cargo, and industrial materials.
