About Weight Units

The gram (g) is a metric unit of weight defined as one thousandth of a kilogram. It's widely used for measuring small masses in cooking, laboratory work, and everyday measurements.

The kilogram (kg) is the base unit of mass in the International System of Units (SI). It's defined by the Planck constant, which is exactly 6.62607015×10−34 joule-seconds. One kilogram equals 1000 grams.
