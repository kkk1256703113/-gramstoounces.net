About Weight Units

The gram (g) is a metric unit of weight defined as one thousandth of a kilogram. It's widely used in scientific contexts and in most countries worldwide.

The grain (gr) is a unit of weight equal to 64.79891 milligrams. It is one of the earliest units of mass and is the smallest unit in the troy, avoirdupois, and apothecaries' systems.
